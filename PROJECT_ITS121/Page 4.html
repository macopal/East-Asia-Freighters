Understanding Shipping Documents

Shipping documents such as the Bill of Lading, Arrival Notice, and Letter of Credit can be complex. We simplify these essential documents to help you understand their purpose and requirements.

Document Description
Commercial Invoice Issued by the exporter to the importer, this invoice is essential for customs clearance.
Packing List Provides detailed cargo information, packaging methods, and labeling details.
Customs Declaration Specifies import/export goods and is crucial for calculating duties and taxes.
Bill of Lading & Sea Waybill Acts as a transport contract and proof of cargo receipt, while a Sea Waybill serves as a non-negotiable receipt.
Certificates Includes Production, Vessel, and Phytosanitary Certificates required for specific cargo types.
Air or Rail Waybill Acknowledges cargo possession for air or rail transport but lacks the negotiability of a Bill of Lading.
Arrival Notice Notifies recipients of cargo arrival, providing shipment details and customs information.
Certificate of Origin Declares the manufacturing country and is used to determine import eligibility and duties.
Importer Security Filing Required for U.S. ocean cargo imports, providing shipment details to customs before departure.
Letter of Credit A bank guarantee ensuring payment to the exporter upon submission of required documents.
